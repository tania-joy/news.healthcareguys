    How to choose machine learning algorithms

    <p>According to Andrew&nbsp;Ng (from Coursera), Machine learning is the science of getting computers to act without being explicitly programmed.</p><p>When we review machine learning we see that there are basically three types of machine learning. Supervised, UnSupervised &amp; Reinforcement.&nbsp; If we take the example of supervised learning, we solve problems/predict where we know input &amp; label (Target) ahead. In the case of classification problem, we use different algorithms Naive Bayes, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Random Decision Forests.And we have linear regression, logistic regression.&nbsp; The neural network is another candidate (example Gradient Descent).</p><p>&nbsp;Being said, along with choosing which programming language &amp; platform we choose, it is equally important to know which algorithm works perfectly for our problem.</p><p>The answer to the question "What machine learning algorithm should I use?" is always "It depends." It depends on how the math of the algorithm was translated into instructions for the computer you are using. And it depends on how much time you have.     What is the GDPR, its requirements and deadlines?

    <p>GDPR ( General Data Protection Regulation) is an EU regulation that intends to provide protection to personal data of the EU citizens and increase the constraints on organizations who handle these personal data. GDPR law applies to all companies that collect and process data belonging to EU citizens. Its major concern is data protection for individuals within the European Union and manages the export of personal data outside the EU. The primary objective of GDPR is to give citizens back control of their personal data. It will come into action on <b>25th May 2018</b>.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><div style="-en-clipboard:true;"><span style="font-size: 1rem;">There are two major parts of the Regulation that are to be highlighted:</span><br></div><div><br></div><p></p><ul><li>&nbsp; &nbsp; The GDPR will be applicable to all companies that control or process data of EU citizens even if they are based outside of the EU.</li><li>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The penalties for not following the terms and conditions of the Regulation are going to be severe. Based on the type and severity of the violation, organizations will be liable to pay fines of up to €20 million or 4% of their global annual revenue which is something they cannot afford.</li></ul><p>This article gives us an overview about what is GDPR , How it affect us and What changes should be made soon etc.</p> More >>
